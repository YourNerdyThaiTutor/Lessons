Lesson 3 Tones

There are 5 tones in Thai.

We'll start with the most basic rule

Again, just like how we started with middle toned consonants, long vowels have straight forward rules, for now. You will stretch out the sounds of these vowels.

  • See tones table

The following are the base vowels that have long pronunciation (The dashes are there as place holder for a consonant)

-า (a)

It makes "a" sound like in English, as in "baah", "barn", "yarn", etc. It goes to the right of the consonant.

  • Practice

-ี (ee)

It makes "ee" sound like in English, as in "bee", "meet", "see", etc. It goes on top of the consonant.

  • Practice

-ู (oo)

It makes "oo" sound like in English, as in "boo", "zoo", "moon", etc. It goes below the consonant.

  • Practice

เ- (a)

It makes "a" sound like in English but different from -า, it is the "a" as in "say", "pay", "may", etc. It goes in front (to the left) of the consonant.

  • Practice

แ- (a)

This one also makes "a" sound like in English but different from -า and เ-, it is the "a" as in "sad", "mad", "map", etc. It goes in front (to the left) of the consonant.

  • Practice

โ- (o)

This one also makes "o" sound like in English, it is the "o" as in "so", "mow", "dough", etc. It goes in front (to the left) of the consonant.

  • Practice
