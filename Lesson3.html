Lesson 3 Tones

There are 5 tones in Thai.

We'll start with the most basic rule when tones are applied to mid toned consonants.

Tones are placed right on top of the consonant or a vowel if the vowel goes on top of the consonant

neutral first tone second tone third tone fourth tone
Example กา ก่า ก้า ก๊า ก๋า
Explanation monotone deep and low high clacking crow sounds like you're a sreaming crow being captured you've been released so now you're confused

There are 2 ways to write tones

On the consonant

ป่า

This word means forest, the woods, jungle.

ป้า

This word means aunty

ต่อ

This word means a wasp, or to extend something

ต้อ

This word means cataract or glaucoma on your eyes.

**Use your tones correctly or the meaning of the word can change**

On the vowel

Only when the vowel is on top of the main consonant.

กี่

This word means how many

จี่

This word means to grill or toast

จี้

This word means to mug someone, to poke, or to tickle
