Lesson 34 Months in the year

Do not miss your appointments.

In Thai, it's so easy to tell if a month has 31 days or 30 days. If it ends in ยน, it has 30 days. If it ends in คม, it has 31 days. Being a private tutor in America, I'm so tempted to teach the little 10-year-olds some Thai, so they can know what month has how many days. It's so rough nowadays trying to get these kids to be literate, like why am I teaching you English??? It's not even my first language.

When people are talking casually, they don't really put the คม,ยน,พันธ์ at the end. You can shorten it once you get all of the words down tight.

Example sentences

sentence translation pronunciation
วันที่ห้ามิถุนาไปสวนสัตว์กันมั้ย On June 5th, do you want to go to the zoo together?
ห้างเปิดวันที่สิบสองกันยาค่ะ The mall opens on September 12th.
มีวันที่ยี่สิบสองกรกฎาว่างค่ะ We have July 22nd available.
วันจันทร์ที่สี่เมษาต้องไปโรงพยาบาล Monday, April 4th, I have to go to the hospital.
ต้องไปธนาคารก่อนวันที่เจ็ดมีนา Have to go to the bank before March 7th.
เมื่อวันที่สิบหกธันวาเขาไปโรงพักมา On December 6th, he went to the police station.
จองร้านอาหารไว้วันที่สิบเก้าพฤศจิกา I booked a restaurant for the 19th of November.

As you can see in a lot of examples, when people are talking conversationally, they don't really put the คม,ยน,พันธ์ at the end. You can shorten it, because Thai people don't believe in full government names of anyone or anything anyway.
