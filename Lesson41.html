Lesson 41 Emergencies

It's hard trying to keep ourselves alive.

Things happens. Just memorize these and yell the one you need at the moment off the top of your lungs. Don't even worry about grammars, get the important vocab in and survive.

Example sentences

sentence translation pronunciation
ต้องไปห้องฉุกเฉิน I have to go to the emergency room
เรียกรถพยาบาลให้หน่อยค่ะ Can you call the ambulance for me?
ระวังรถ Watch out for cars!
เรียกตำรวจให้หน่อยค่ะ Can you call the police for me?
แถวนี้มีร้านขายยามั้ยคะ Is there a pharmacy around here?
กระเป๋าตังค์หาย My wallet is lost!
โดนทำร้ายร่างกายมา I was assulted.
โทรศัพท์โดนขโมย My phone was stolen.
ไปสถานทูตญี่ปุ่นยังไงคะ How do I get to the Japanese Embassy?
แถวซอยนี้ปลอดภัยมั้ยคะ Is around this alley safe?
หาแผนที่ตรงไหนได้คะ Where can I find a map?

As you can see in a lot of examples, Thai people tend to skip the pronouns when talking about first or second person, only about third person if any. You can add in the pronouns, which we'll cover later, but it wouldn't sound natural to Thai people.
