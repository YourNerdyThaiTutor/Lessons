Lesson 42 Pronouns

Finally, you may have pronouns.

Pronouns in Thai are...complicated. It has a lot of levels on who you can use them with. You don't have to strictly follow these rules if you want to have some linguistic creative freedom, but here are the guidelines.

Formal ceremonial polite

These are the pronouns you use in a formal setting, like in court, government documents, meeting royalties, public events, etc. However, if you want to be sarcastic and use it in a casual setting to portray that the person you are talking to feels like they are superior to you, you are also welcome to use these. My dad uses ข้าพเจ้า to refer to himself all the time when he talks to my mom. I didn't realize it was weird until my sister pointed it out.

As you can see in a lot of examples, Thai people tend to skip the pronouns when talking about first or second person, only about third person if any. You can add in the pronouns, which we'll cover later, but it wouldn't sound natural to Thai people.
